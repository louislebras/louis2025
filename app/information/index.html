Louis Le Bras is a designer, front-end engineer and psychology researcher in neuroscience and monetary systems.


He is the creator of Principle OS™, the culmination of nearly a decade of research, experimentation, and production across design systems, front-end engineering, content architectures, and automation. Principle OS™ a radical operating system to craft everything on the web in few days with AI with high customization including design systems engineering and more: to bring to life and materialize the ideas he envisions without limits, with his will as the only obstacle.

Alongside this work, he founded Archives Bottega®, an independent curatorial platform dedicated to coffee machines, tools, places, and rituals. Archives Bottega applies the same logic of structural clarity and long-term thinking to physical objects and daily practices, documenting tools chosen for their engineering, durability, and relevance over time.

He has been working with brands, agencies and startups for almost 9 years cumulated. Selected — Nike, Kitchen Aid, FFF, Fraser Yachts... and had the pleasure to challenge them in their conversion system, client journey and brand experience.

About
Beyond design and engineering, Louis explores long-term thinking around ownership, durability, and autonomy in systems: whether digital, physical, or economic. His work focuses on building tools, platforms, and frameworks that favor clarity, resilience, and independence over short-term optimization.

His approach consistently bridges craft and systems: understanding how objects are used over time, how interfaces shape behavior, and how well-designed structures can support autonomy rather than dependency.
